Privacy Policy

How we collect, use, and protect information when you use SourceAbility’s website and services.

Last updated: April 10, 2026

This policy is provided for general information. It is not legal advice. SourceAbility, Inc. (“SourceAbility,” “we,” “us,” or “our”) encourages you to consult qualified counsel regarding your specific obligations. We may update this page from time to time; the “Last updated” date will change when we do.

Who we are

SourceAbility, Inc. is an executive recruiting firm focused on data, analytics, AI, and related technology roles. Our website (sourceabilityinc.com) describes our services and allows employers, candidates, and partners to contact us.

Scope

This Privacy Policy applies to information we collect through our website, email, and related online interactions with our firm. It does not govern third-party websites (for example, job boards or social networks) that we may link to; those sites have their own policies.

Information we collect

Depending on how you interact with us, we may collect:

  • Contact and inquiry data. When you submit our contact form or email us, we receive information you provide (such as name, email address, phone number, company, role, and message content).
  • Recruiting-related information. If you engage with us as a candidate, client, or partner, we may collect professional details you share (such as resume or profile information, employment history, and compensation expectations) as part of providing recruiting services.
  • Technical data. Like most sites, our servers and analytics tools may log information such as IP address, browser type, device type, referring URLs, and pages viewed. We use this to operate and improve the site and to understand aggregate usage.
  • Cookies and similar technologies. We may use cookies or similar technologies for essential site functionality, preferences, or analytics. You can control cookies through your browser settings.

How we use information

We use the information above to:

  • Respond to inquiries and provide recruiting and related services;
  • Operate, secure, and improve our website and communications;
  • Comply with law, enforce our agreements, and protect rights and safety;
  • Send operational messages (for example, replies to your inquiry). We do not sell your personal information.

Form submissions and service providers

Our website contact form is delivered through a third-party form provider (for example, Web3Forms or a comparable service) that processes submissions on our behalf. That provider receives the data you submit in the form in order to route messages to us. Their use of data is subject to their terms and privacy policy as well as our instructions for providing the service.

Sharing of information

We do not sell your personal information. We may share information with service providers who assist us (such as hosting, email delivery, form processing, or IT security), with professional advisers where appropriate, or when required by law, subpoena, or legal process. We may also share information in connection with a merger, acquisition, or sale of assets, subject to appropriate confidentiality safeguards.

Retention

We retain information for as long as needed to fulfill the purposes described in this policy, meet legal and professional obligations, and resolve disputes. Retention periods vary depending on the nature of the data and applicable law.

Security

We implement reasonable administrative, technical, and organizational measures designed to protect information against unauthorized access, loss, or misuse. No method of transmission over the Internet is completely secure; we cannot guarantee absolute security.

Your choices and rights

Depending on where you live, you may have rights regarding your personal information (for example, to access, correct, delete, or restrict certain processing, or to opt out of certain disclosures). To make a request, contact us at info@sourceabilityinc.com. We will respond consistent with applicable law. California residents may have additional rights under the CCPA/CPRA; if you are a California resident, you may contact us using the same address to exercise those rights where applicable.

Children’s privacy

Our services are not directed to children under 16, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If you believe we have collected information from a child, please contact us and we will take appropriate steps.

International visitors

We are based in the United States. If you access our site from outside the United States, you understand that your information may be processed in the U.S. and other jurisdictions where we or our providers operate.

Changes to this policy

We may update this Privacy Policy periodically. We will post the revised version on this page and update the “Last updated” date. Continued use of the site after changes constitutes your acknowledgment of the updated policy, to the extent permitted by law.
